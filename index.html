The Problem

You're not a bad plant parent.
You just can't see underground.

The number one way people kill houseplants is overwatering. The number two way is forgetting to water. Both problems share the same cause — you cannot see what is happening at the root level until something goes wrong.

"By the time something looks wrong, it usually already is."

Analog meters give you a vague number with no context. Bluetooth sensors give you an app and a battery that dies in three weeks. Neither one tells you what to actually do for your specific plant. You're left guessing — and your plants pay for it.

#1 Overwatering kills more houseplants than neglect
